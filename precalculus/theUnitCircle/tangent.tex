Tangent is the quotient of sine and cosine.

\[   \tan(\theta)  =  \frac{\sin(\theta)}{\cos(\theta)}  \]


\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{zeros:} $\tan(\theta)$ has a zero everywhere that $\sin(\theta)$ has a zero:  all of the whole-$\pi$'s.
\item \textbf{singluaries:} $\tan(\theta)$ has a singularity everywhere that $\cos(\theta)$ has a zero:  all of the half-$\pi$'s.
\end{itemize}

This means the graph has intercepts at the whole-$\pi$'s and vertical asymptotes at the half-$\pi$'s.

\begin{itemize}
\item Tangent is always increasing.
\item Tangent has no maximums or minimum, since it is unbounded near the half-$\pi$'s.
\item The period for tangent is $\pi$.
\item the principal interval is $\left[ -\frac{\pi}{2}, \frac{\pi}{2} \right)$
\end{itemize}















\subsection*{Analysis}



















\begin{example} Tangent

Describe the behavior of   $T(y) = \tan\left( 2 y + \frac{\pi}{2} \right)$



\textbf{\textcolor{red!75!green}{explanation}} 



The ``angle'' is $2 y + \frac{\pi}{2}$.

We want to know when $2 y + \frac{\pi}{2}$ has the values $-\frac{\pi}{2}$, $0$, and $\frac{\pi}{2}$.






\[
\begin{array}{l|l}
\text{angle}                          & y  \\ \hline
2 y + \frac{\pi}{2} = -\frac{\pi}{2}        & y = -\frac{\pi}{2}  \\
2 y + \frac{\pi}{2} = 0               &  y = -\frac{\pi}{4} \\
2 y + \frac{\pi}{2} = \frac{\pi}{2}   & y = 0  \\
\end{array}
\]






Our principal interval is $\left( -\frac{\pi}{2}, 0 \right)$.

That makes the period $0 - \left( -\frac{\pi}{2} \right) = \frac{\pi}{2}$.




The tangent function function grows from $\infty$ to $\infty$ as its angle moves from $-\frac{\pi}{2}$ to $\frac{\pi}{2}$.

For $T$, this would corerespond to $y$ moving from $-\frac{\pi}{2}$ to $0$.

This means $T$ is an increasing function.



All of this repeats with a period of $\frac{\pi}{2}$.

\textbf{\textcolor{blue!55!black}{Extrema}} 


Tangent functions do not have extrema values.  They are unbounded.




\textbf{\textcolor{blue!55!black}{Range}} 

The range of all tangent functions is $(-\infty, \infty)$.


\end{example}







































\begin{example} Tangent

Describe the behavior of   $W(k) = \tan\left( \frac{\pi}{4} - \frac{k}{4} \right)$



\textbf{\textcolor{red!75!green}{explanation}} 



The ``angle'' is $\frac{\pi}{4} - \frac{k}{4}$.

We want to know when $\frac{\pi}{4} - \frac{k}{4}$ has the values $-\frac{\pi}{2}$, $0$, and $\frac{\pi}{2}$.






\[
\begin{array}{l|l}
\text{angle}                          & K  \\ \hline
\frac{\pi}{4} - \frac{k}{4} = -\frac{\pi}{2}        & k = 3 \pi  \\
\frac{\pi}{4} - \frac{k}{4} = 0               &  k = \pi \\
\frac{\pi}{4} - \frac{k}{4} = \frac{\pi}{2}   & k = -\pi  \\
\end{array}
\]






Our principal interval is $\left( -\pi, 3 \pi \right)$.

That makes the period $3 \pi - (-\pi) = 4 \pi$.




The tangent function function grows from $\infty$ to $\infty$ as its angle moves from $-\frac{\pi}{2}$ to $\frac{\pi}{2}$.

For $W$, this would corerespond to $k$ moving from $3 \pi$ to $-\pi$.

The function and the domain change oppositely. This means $W$ is a decreasing function.



All of this repeats with a period of $4 \pi$.

The range of tangent functions is $(-\infty, \infty)$.


\end{example}

In the diagram above, we know that $a = \cos(\theta)$ and $b = \sin(\theta)$.

We also know the unit circle right triangles are both similar to the big right triangle, since they all include the angle $\theta$.

Since, they are similar, we know that 


\[    \frac{opp}{adj} = \frac{b}{a}  = \frac{\sin(\theta)}{\cos(\theta)}   = \frac{h}{1}          \]


\[         \tan(\theta) = h\]




$\tan(\theta)$ can be represented as the length of the tangent line segment, from the tangent point to the $x$-axis.
