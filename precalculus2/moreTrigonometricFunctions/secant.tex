Secant is the reciprocal of cosine. 

\[   \sec(\theta)  =  \frac{1}{\cos(\theta)}  \]



\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{zeros:} $\sec(\theta)$ has no zeros, because cosine has no singularities.
\item \textbf{singluaries:} $\sec(\theta)$ has a singularity everywhere that $\cos(\theta)$ has a zero:  all of the half-$\pi$'s.
\end{itemize}

This means no intercepts and vertical asymptotes at the half-$\pi$'s.

In the diagram above, we know that $a = \cos(\theta)$ and $b = \sin(\theta)$.


$a+c$ is the hypotenuse of a right triangle that is similar to the unit circle right triangle.





\[    \frac{hyp}{opp} = \frac{a+c}{1}   = \frac{1}{\cos(\theta)}       \]


\[         a+c = \sec(\theta)\]
