Points on the Unit Circle have two coordinates, which each depend on the angle the radius makes with the positive $x$-axis.  In other words, the coordinates are funcitons of the angle.  

We call these functions sine and cosine.

The coordinates are $( sin(\theta), cos(\theta) )$.

From these two functions, we can also create a third, tangent.

\[       
tan(\theta) = \frac{sin(\theta)}{cos(\theta)}
\]



As functions, sine, cosine, and tangent have graphs, characteristics, and features.
